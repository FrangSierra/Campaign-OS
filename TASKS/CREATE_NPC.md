# Create NPC

## Purpose

Turn a scene need or a loose character idea into a grounded NPC design without confusing creative material with campaign canon.

## Procedure

1. Establish the campaign, planning scope, and whether the user wants a quick concept, a guided interview, or full delegation.
2. Load the smallest relevant campaign context: current location, active factions, party situation, existing NPCs, and the plan or mystery that creates the need.
3. Apply the `entity-extractor` duplicate-check approach before inventing a new person. Recommend reuse or reframing when it serves the scene better.
4. Invite optional answers about:
   - role in the scene and relationship to the party;
   - attitude, values, and immediate want;
   - voice, speech habits, expression, and physical presence;
   - age, ancestry, culture, class, and presentation;
   - useful resources, boundaries, secrets, or recurring potential.
5. Let the user skip any field or delegate it to the operator. Never make unanswered questions block progress.
6. Produce a design packet that separates confirmed anchors, supported context, and proposed creative details.
7. Offer a small set of introduction beats or hooks only when useful. Mark them as working prep and route any future consequence ideas to `consequence-engine` or `session-prep`.
8. Persist the result only with explicit user direction, and only in `WORKING/` until play and approval establish canon.

## Output Shape

- scene function
- confirmed anchors
- campaign grounding and duplicate check
- attitude and social posture
- voice and expression
- appearance and practical details
- wants, limits, and likely withholdings
- relationship to the party
- introduction options
- flexible details and open questions

## Failure

- an NPC is designed without checking the relevant campaign context;
- operator invention is presented as a user-confirmed fact;
- a proposed hook is written as an event that already happened;
- the design is automatically promoted into canon.
