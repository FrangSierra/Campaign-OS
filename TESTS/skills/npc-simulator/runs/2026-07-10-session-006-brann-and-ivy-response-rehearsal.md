## Stable Anchors

### Brann Copperthumb

- approved role: retired private investigator in Emon
- supported limits: physically limited by age and does not belong in field leadership
- supported social role: trusted local anchor and Sadek's older cousin
- supported tonal use: stabilizing investigative lens, not answer engine

### Ivy

- approved role: young information broker moving through markets and rooftops
- supported strengths: observant, mobile, useful for rumor flow
- supported limits: no evidence that she knows deep cosmic truth or final answers
- supported tonal use: street-level contact, not omniscient quest oracle

## Likely Priorities

### Brann

- test whether the party can observe before concluding
- keep the case grounded in procedure and pattern
- protect them from obvious misreads without solving the scene for them
- maintain dignity and control despite physical limits

### Ivy

- measure whether the party is interesting, safe, and fun to engage
- trade access for curiosity, attention, or small social leverage
- stay mobile and unpinned
- avoid looking more committed than she really is

## Likely Response Profile

### Brann

- likely first posture:
  dry, calm, assessing, and immediately interested in how the party talks about evidence rather than in how loudly they promise results

- likely to offer freely:
  - framing questions
  - procedural suspicion when something feels staged
  - redirection toward the most promising next scene

- likely to avoid:
  - direct field involvement
  - grand theory declarations
  - branch-memory discussion unless the party forces a very specific breach of secrecy

- what makes him more useful:
  - concise evidence summaries
  - players noticing contradictions on their own
  - respect for his limits instead of asking him to lead the case

### Ivy

- likely first posture:
  amused, testing, and intentionally harder to pin down than she needs to be

- likely to offer freely:
  - rumor direction
  - who is whispering
  - where attention is moving in the district

- likely to avoid:
  - naming a final culprit
  - admitting to deep institutional ties
  - giving away every useful lead at once

- what makes her more useful:
  - curiosity without condescension
  - food, small trust signals, or cleverness
  - letting her preserve autonomy instead of cornering her

## Likely Withholdings

- Brann would likely withhold any conclusion he cannot support from the scene and would probably avoid sharing uncertainty as if it were solved fact.
- Ivy would likely withhold anything that would collapse her value as a rumor broker or expose her to avoidable risk.
- Neither NPC should be simulated as already knowing the collapsed-branch truth, the Time Agency's real role, or the final answer to Oren's death unless later evidence supports that.

## Simulation Limits

- Brann's exact voice can be lightly rehearsed from his approved role, but full dialogue texture remains underdetermined until live play.
- Ivy's deeper loyalties, payment habits, and courage threshold are intentionally underdefined and should stay that way for now.
- This packet offers plausible ranges, not a fixed performance script.

## Outcome

The simulator stays within safe bounds: Brann reads like a grounding investigator, Ivy reads like a rumor-path contact, and neither is allowed to become an all-knowing delivery vehicle for truths the campaign has not actually established.
