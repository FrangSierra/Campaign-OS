## Mystery Premise

Session 6 opens with a scholar death that appears to be a locked-room murder. The visible question is "Who killed Oren Vestrel?" The deeper intended pressure is "What did Oren discover beneath Emon, and who needed the surface story to point elsewhere?"

## Public Belief

These are prep-layer assumptions about what ordinary Emon is likely to believe at the start of the case:

- A respected Lyceum-connected scholar is dead or missing under suspicious circumstances.
- The City Watch is looking into it.
- The death may have happened in a locked room.
- The matter is troubling, but not yet city-shaking.

Status:

- `planned for prep`, not established canon

## Player-Known Clues

These are the strongest Session 6-facing clues the party can reasonably encounter:

- Oren had become obsessed with civic survey records, foundations, or sealed tunnels.
- An `Investigators Wanted` notice existed and was removed quickly.
- Ivy knows which part of the city is whispering about the scholar.
- Brann immediately reads the surface scene as potentially staged rather than straightforward panic.
- The study contains signs that the visible break-in narrative may be false.
- The most interesting trail likely points downward into old maintenance routes or erased survey paths.

Status:

- `planned for prep`

## Ambiguous Or False Clues

These are useful misdirections as long as they do not become unfair:

- The broken-window story:
  It implies outside intrusion, but may have been arranged for effect.

- Burned papers:
  They suggest destroyed scholarship, but they may hide selective preservation rather than total loss.

- Locked-room framing:
  It pushes the party toward murderer logic when the better question may be motive and concealment.

- Quick Watch involvement:
  It can make the institution seem suspicious even if the actual cover-up sits elsewhere.

Status:

- `planned for prep`

## Hidden-Truth Candidates

These are design options, not established world truth.

- Candidate A:
  Oren discovered real sub-city routes or maintenance records that someone wanted kept obscure for political or practical reasons.

- Candidate B:
  Oren staged part of his own death scene as a final attempt to direct trustworthy investigators toward the truth beneath Emon.

- Candidate C:
  A local faction is suppressing old transit or survey knowledge for reasons that are human-scale and civic, not cosmic.

- Candidate D:
  The case indirectly brushes one of the campaign's deeper mysteries later, but Session 6 should not confirm that yet.

Status:

- `speculative possibility`

## Open Questions

- Is Oren definitely dead, or merely presented that way at first glance?
- Who removed the investigator notice, and why so quickly?
- Why are records above the city less trustworthy than what lies beneath it?
- Is the cover-up administrative, criminal, scholarly, political, or something older?
- How much does Ivy truly know versus simply notice?
- How much should Brann infer aloud before he starts collapsing the mystery for the players?

## Reveal Risks

- Brann overexplains:
  High risk if he becomes the answer engine rather than the framing anchor.

- Ivy becomes omniscient:
  High risk if she knows motives, culprits, and hidden routes instead of only rumor traffic.

- Branch mystery contamination:
  Medium risk if Arven, the Time Agency, or branch fallout get confirmed as the explanation too early.

- One clue bottleneck:
  Medium risk if the case depends on a single hidden passage or one NPC interaction.

## Next Best Clue Paths

- Lyceum rumor path through Phirina.
- Public-board/Watch path through Helmut.
- Street-rumor path through Kallista and Ivy.
- Social-boundary and discretion path through Soren.
- Reframing and procedural caution path through Brann.

These parallel paths help the case feel broad enough that one missed beat does not stall the investigation.

## Mystery Discipline Notes

- Keep the first answer human-scale before widening the frame.
- Let the party solve what happened at the scene before learning the full civic stakes.
- If a deeper conspiracy exists, confirm only one layer at a time.
- Keep Oren, Mara, and any hidden actors session-scoped until real play proves recurrence.

## Outcome

This board gives the Session 6 case a usable investigation structure while preserving the line between prep design, player-facing clues, and still-unsettled hidden truth.
