## Strengths

- The campaign survived a high-complexity prologue reset without losing its truth boundaries.
- The party has strong individual identity, vivid carryover consequences, and a clear shared reason to care about the restored world.
- Session 6 prep gives Emon a credible path to becoming a lived home base instead of a mere destination.
- Brann and Ivy provide the first meaningful expansion of the recurring NPC bench without overpopulating the repo too early.
- Recent QA, mystery, and consequence passes are aligned with each other rather than fighting over tone.

## Stress Points

- Most active mysteries still point back toward the prologue branch, Arven, or the Time Agency. That is fine for now, but Emon needs at least one grounded success before those forces return to center stage.
- The recurring cast is still narrow. Brann and Ivy help, but the campaign would benefit from one more civic-facing or institutional NPC who is neither mentor nor child broker.
- Many important places remain state-level only. That is not broken, but it means Emon's long-term texture still depends heavily on future sessions rather than existing durable location records.

## Watch List

- `Over-centralization risk`
  Keep the Oren Vestrel case from becoming instant proof that every city institution hides the same master conspiracy.

- `Branch-memory secrecy fatigue`
  The private memory boundary is compelling now, but it will need emotional payoff later or it may become pure maintenance burden.

- `Recurring NPC distribution`
  Avoid leaning on Brann for every formal clue and Ivy for every informal clue.

- `Home-base attachment`
  Session 6 should show why Emon is worth returning to even when no cosmic threat is active.

## Next Maintenance Checks

- Re-run `campaign-health` after live Session 6 to see whether Emon earned attachment beyond mystery utility.
- Use `campaign-qa` and `knowledge-matrix` together if branch-memory secrecy starts touching new allies.
- Consider a future `session-prep` packet that emphasizes one ordinary city relationship or location payoff before the next Time Agency pull.

## Outcome

The campaign is in a good state. Its main need is not repair but successful transition: Emon must become emotionally sticky and socially textured before the deeper prologue-scale pressures reclaim the center of play.
