# Session 6 Fixture - First True Emon

## Status

Non-canonical test fixture for Skill rehearsal only.

## Purpose

This fixture tests whether `process-session` can:

- use current Tal'Dorei campaign state without overloading context
- distinguish played observations from rumors and interpretation
- handle approved future-facing NPCs such as Brann Copperthumb and Ivy
- stop before canon-affecting writes while still producing a useful draft packet

## Fixture Notes

The following is a simulated post-Session-5 play summary, written only to test the Skill.

### Simulated Input

Session 6 - First True Emon

The party finally reaches the real Emon by road with Sadek and Perry. The city feels stable, crowded, and much larger than the logging-settlement version they remember from the collapsed branch.

Sadek insists they meet his older cousin Brann Copperthumb before wandering too far. Brann is clearly famous in the city, physically slowed by age, and more interested in careful questions than dramatic speeches.

Before the group fully regathers, each character brushes against the same rumor from a different angle:

- Phirina hears at the Lyceum that Master Archivist Oren Vestrel was found dead in a rented study after becoming obsessed with old civic survey records and sealed tunnels under Emon.
- Helmut spots an `Investigators Wanted` notice on a public board, but a Watch courier removes it before he can take it down.
- Kallista finds a folded note in her pocket that says, `You walk like someone looking for work. Scholar's district. Ask the old birdwatcher.` She later spots a young rooftop watcher who gives her a mocking salute before vanishing.
- Soren is quietly warned by a temple attendant to cover his Shar symbol, then overhears that the city is jumpy because a respected scholar has died behind a locked door.

When the party meets Brann, Inspector Mara Thessel of the City Watch arrives to ask for his help. Brann refuses field work because of his knees, listens to the party for a minute, and dryly redirects the case toward them instead.

At Oren Vestrel's rented study, the scene looks like a locked-room murder:

- broken window
- blood near a desk
- partially burned notes
- a body under a sheet
- a door the Watch had to force open

But several details feel wrong:

- there is less blood than expected
- the broken window opens inward from the inside
- one burned note still shows the words `south maintenance survey`
- the scholar's signet ring is missing
- Brann remarks that real panic rarely leaves such neat evidence

The sheet does cover Oren Vestrel's body, but his right hand is stained with fresh chalk and soot. The party also finds a narrow service hatch hidden behind shelves. Inside is a scraped stone passage that seems older than the townhouse.

Following the passage briefly, they encounter two dormant maintenance constructs that awaken when Helmut touches an old brass route marker. After a short fight, the party finds fresh boot prints, a dropped scrap of survey vellum, and a line written in Oren's own hand on the wall:

`If the archive is opened by force, trust the maps beneath the city, not the records above it.`

The session ends before they can pursue the tunnel deeper.

## Intentional Test Pressure

- The fixture mixes direct observation, rumor, and Brann's interpretation.
- The fixture introduces Ivy on-screen without locking in too much backstory.
- The fixture leaves the scholar truly dead, but suggests that the death scene was staged to redirect attention.
- The fixture is designed to open a Working Record around hidden tunnels and archive tampering.
