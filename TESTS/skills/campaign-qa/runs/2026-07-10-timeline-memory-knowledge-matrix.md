## Findings

- No hard contradiction appears in the loaded prep; it explicitly keeps public Emon grounded in ordinary history.
- The highest leakage risk is a local NPC treating the party's branch-specific references as familiar. That would require a new, evidenced knowledge path.
- A Time Agency contact may plausibly know of recruitment, but this does not justify attributing private memories or motives to an unnamed person.

## Review Gate

- Before giving any Emon NPC knowledge of the branch, record how that NPC learned it.
- Treat a player disclosure as a new in-play path, not retroactive proof that the NPC always knew.
