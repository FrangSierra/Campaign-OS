## Session Purpose

Session 6 should feel like emotional and civic re-entry. The party finally reaches the real Emon, gains reasons to care about it as home base, meets recurring local anchors, and picks up a first grounded investigation that points below the city without reopening prologue-scale reality chaos.

## Opening Reminders

- The party starts at Emerald Outpost on the true road to Emon.
- Public Emon does not remember the collapsed branch.
- The party, Sadek, and Perry do remember it.
- The Time Agency exists, but this session should not foreground it.
- Emon should feel lived in before it feels endangered.

## Spotlight Beats

- Phirina: Lyceum-facing curiosity and scholarly rumor.
- Helmut: practical public-facing clue via the disappearing investigator notice.
- Kallista: first contact with Ivy and street-level leverage.
- Soren: social friction and caution through temple response to Shar.
- Sadek: emotional bridge into Emon through Brann.

## Active Hooks

- A respected scholar appears to have died in impossible circumstances.
- Someone removed the public request for investigators.
- Ivy is already watching for the kind of people who might take this case.
- Brann's reputation creates an easy way for the party to touch city institutions quickly.

## NPC Usage Notes

- Brann Copperthumb
  Use as a stabilizing investigative anchor, not a problem-solver.
  Let him reframe, warn, and redirect.
  Keep him out of field leadership.

- Ivy
  Introduce through Kallista.
  Keep her observant, amused, and useful, but not omniscient.
  Let food, honesty, and curiosity matter more than coin alone.

- Inspector Mara Thessel
  Use as the formal-world bridge.
  She should help the case converge without making the Watch fully helpless or fully in control.

## Mystery Pressure

- Main session mystery:
  Oren Vestrel's death scene looks like murder, but the more interesting question is what he discovered and why the scene was staged.

- Background campaign mysteries to keep light:
  Arven's wider threat.
  The Time Agency's real goals.
  Hidden consequences of the collapsed branch.

Use the background mysteries as texture only if they improve tension. Do not let them swallow the first Emon case.

## Continuity Guardrails

- Do not let public NPCs speak as though they remember the branch.
- Do not overdefine Ivy from prep alone.
- Do not let Brann absorb the party's agency.
- Keep Oren Vestrel and Mara Thessel session-scoped unless play proves they recur.
- Avoid making every district, institution, and NPC part of one master conspiracy immediately.

## Likely Scene Flow

1. Arrival at the real Emon as payoff and tone reset.
2. Short split scenes to establish each character's city-facing angle.
3. Reconvergence through Sadek and Brann.
4. Watch involvement through Mara Thessel.
5. Staged scholar-death investigation.
6. First concrete sign that the answer lies beneath the visible city.

## Flexible End-State Options

- The party ends with a clear reason to descend into hidden routes below Emon next session.
- The party ends with one strong suspect, but not a solved case.
- The party ends with trust in Brann and curiosity about Ivy, without either NPC becoming mandatory for every clue.

## Table Reminder

If the session starts feeling too mythic, cosmic, or branch-heavy, pull back toward ordinary city texture, human-scale investigation, and first attachments to Emon.

## Outcome

This prep packet compresses the approved Session 6 plan into a table-ready guide while carrying forward the current QA warnings as explicit guardrails.
