  Session 5 - The Rune Breaks (Summary)

  Purpose
  The party reaches the heart of the ancient ruins, confronts Vael Thamior, uncovers the true mastermind behind the temporal catastrophe, and becomes recruited by the Time Agency after surviving the collapse of reality itself.

  The Undercity
  Beyond the massive stone gate lies an enormous underground civilization built jointly by dwarves and elves.
  The city surrounds an impossible vertical chasm filled with crystal structures, suspended bridges, upside-down towers, and fragments of the mysterious Rune carved into every surface.
  Throughout their exploration, reality continues to behave unnaturally:
  - Time briefly flows backwards.
  - Reflections show different versions of the party.
  - Floating Watcher Eyes silently observe them.
  - Perry constantly comments on how much he dislikes the cave while accidentally revealing useful clues.
  - Sadek translates portions of the ancient dwarven runes, learning that the city was built to contain something rather than worship it.

  Bridge of Unspent Choices
  Crossing an enormous bridge, every party member encounters another version of themselves.
  Each echo represents a life they could have lived and asks a deeply personal question.
  Rather than fighting, every character answers honestly and the echoes peacefully allow them to pass, reinforcing the campaign's recurring theme that personal choices define reality.

  Mirror Chamber
  Inside a chamber filled with magical mirrors, the party witnesses countless versions of Tal'Dorei across different timelines:
  - The true city of Emon.
  - The logging settlement.
  - Ancient dwarves building the containment Rune.
  - Futures that have not yet happened.
  - Their own older selves walking these halls.
  Each player also shares a personal memory reflected by one of the mirrors.
  One cracked mirror reveals Vael Thamior working desperately beside the Rune.
  Watching him, the party learns something crucial:
  Sadek was never supposed to survive.
  His death was considered a fixed point necessary for Vael's calculations.
  By saving Sadek with the Hourglass of Returning, the party unknowingly disrupted the entire correction process.

  The Rune Door
  A final sealed door asks a single question:
  "What survives time?"
  Each character answers sincerely, allowing the door to open.
  Before entering, the party discovers an emergency cache prepared by Vael containing healing potions and supplies for a containment breach.

  Vael Thamior
  At the center of the Rune Chamber, the party finally meets Vael Thamior, Keeper-Archivist of the Rune.
  He is not a lich.
  Instead, he is an exhausted archmage trapped beneath a magical temporal death-mask created by the Rune itself.
  Vael believes:
  - Only one stable timeline can exist.
  - Every failed timeline must be erased.
  - If he stops now, every sacrifice becomes meaningless.
  - Arven showed him how to save reality.
  When Vael notices Sadek still alive, he immediately realizes something impossible has happened.
  His calculations are broken.
  Despite the party's attempts to reason with him, the confrontation inevitably turns violent.

  Battle with Vael
  The party battles Vael while the Rune itself protects him through multiple phases.
  Throughout the fight, the chamber bends time:
  - Reality rewrites itself.
  - Mirrors create magical echoes.
  - The Rune continually attempts to preserve Vael.
  Eventually Helmut's radiant attacks destabilize the temporal mask.
  The mask shatters.
  For the first time, the party sees the real Vael.
  Broken.
  Exhausted.
  Full of regret.
  He admits:
  "That wasn't only me."
  "I let him in."
  Before he can explain further, every Watcher Eye suddenly opens.

  The Paradox Wyrm
  As Vael falls, every Watcher Eye suddenly opens.
  The chained Temporal Agent the party met inside the Mage Tower finally reveals their true identity:
  Arven.
  The prisoner they believed they were helping was never an innocent victim. He had manipulated the party from the very beginning. By weakening the Rune and defeating Vael, they unknowingly freed him from his prison.
  Arven thanks the party.
  The Rune binds itself to Vael's body, transforming him into the Paradox Wyrm, a colossal creature formed from countless collapsed timelines and failed realities.
  The battle becomes impossible through conventional means.

  The Legendary Relics
  The party survives only because of the legendary relics recovered from the Mage Tower.

  Soren
  When the Paradox Wyrm attempts to erase Kallista from existence, Shar grants Soren one final miracle.
  He refuses to let his companion disappear, forcing reality itself to reject her erasure.

  Helmut
  As reality collapses around them, Helmut activates The Last Oath.
  Standing before his companions, he swears:
  "While I keep breathing, no one on my side will fall. So I will defend them until my bones crack."
  Reality itself bends around the strength of his conviction.
  For the remainder of the battle, none of his companions can truly fall while Helmut still stands, allowing the party to survive what should have been an unwinnable fight.

  Phirina
  Phirina sacrifices The Last Page of the Archivist, activating True Revelation.
  For one minute she perceives reality exactly as it truly is.
  She discovers:
  - The dragons are manifestations of collapsed timelines.
  - Vael is merely trapped within the anomaly.
  - Arven has been manipulating events from the shadows all along.

  Kallista
  Time itself freezes.
  Only Arven continues moving.
  He calmly walks through the frozen battlefield before striking Kallista first, revealing that in countless timelines she is always his first victim.
  Kallista activates the Heart of the Mountain.
  Though mortally wounded, she refuses to fall.
  She alone moves through frozen time, reaches Arven, and lands the decisive blow that breaks his control over the Rune.
  His body is destroyed, although he hints that other versions of himself may still exist.

  Collapse of the Rune
  With Arven defeated and the Rune destabilized, reality itself begins collapsing.
  Vael, finally freed from the temporal mask, smiles for the first time.
  His final words are:
  "I remember them."
  "All of them."
  "I'm sorry."
  Everything dissolves into white.

  The White Room
  The party awakens inside an endless white room.
  Waiting for them are two people:
  - An older man with a large moustache quietly reading a newspaper.
  - His secretary.
  The moustached man calmly explains what truly happened.
  When the party crossed the mysterious fog near Emon in Session 1, they unknowingly crossed into an alternate timeline. Every event since then - the logging settlement, the temporal anomalies, the Rune, Vael, and Arven - occurred inside that fractured reality.
  The timeline has now been stabilized.
  The Agency offers the party a choice.
  They can return to their original world, where history remains untouched, or they can also work as independent agents for the Time Agency, taking on missions across unstable timelines whenever needed.
  The party accepts both offers:
  - They will return to their original timeline.
  - They agree to work for the Time Agency when called upon, providing an in-universe explanation for future one-shots or sessions where part of the group is unavailable.

  Ending
  The session ends as the party opens their eyes once again at Emerald Outpost, exactly where their journey originally began.
  This time, however, everything feels right.
  There are no temporal glitches.
  No shifting realities.
  No impossible memories.
  Only the road to Emon stretching ahead.
  The Prologue ends here.
  Session 6 begins the true campaign, with the party finally entering the real Emon for the first time, while the Time Agency remains available as an off-screen framework for future one-shots and side adventures.
