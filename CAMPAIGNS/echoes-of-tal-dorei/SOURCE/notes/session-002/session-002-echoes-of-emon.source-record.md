  Session 2 - Echoes of Emon (Summary)

  Purpose
  Reinforce that the logging settlement is accepted as the real Emon, while the party discovers clues suggesting multiple overlapping realities. Introduce the first major puzzle, the Mage Tower, the Temporal Agent, and the campaign's legendary items.

  Major Events

  Arrival in Emon
  Sadek Bronzebeard struggles with conflicting memories of Emon, insisting there should have been towers before deciding to investigate the surrounding forest. He promises to return 24 hours later.
  Perry continues acting as comic relief, occasionally shouting unsettling phrases like "Wrong road!", "Not again!", and "Where did the city go?"
  Several NPCs casually mention that the market will open later, despite the settlement feeling almost deserted.

  Exploring the Settlement

  Soren & Phirina
  After hearing rumors about Jarek, they search for the mysterious map seller.
  Suddenly, an enormous market appears from nowhere, bustling like the market of a great city.
  Phirina buys a wooden owl carving from a child after remembering the constellation puzzle from Elira's bag (Tree, Owl, Snake, Key).
  Soren nearly burns the child after being mocked before Phirina calms the situation.
  Their relationship grows considerably as they discover they both enjoy researching magic and mysteries together.

  Helmut & Kallista
  They visit the lumberyard looking for work.
  The foreman immediately recognizes them and claims they have been working there for two years.
  Rather than argue, they play along, complete a work shift, receive their wages, and head to the market afterwards.

  Jarek the Map Seller
  The party meets Jarek, an eccentric cartographer whose maps seem almost alive.

  He gifts them:
  A Living Map that continually redraws itself as they travel, adapting to changes in reality.
  A strange coin engraved with a Tree.

  Phirina realizes the Tree symbol matches one of the four constellation symbols found inside Elira's satchel, suggesting they are collecting pieces of a larger puzzle.

  The Laughing Lamia
  The reunited party visits the inn.

  The innkeeper is a younger Mira Voss, apparently from another version of reality.

  She seems to recognize Helmut and especially Kallista, implying Kallista may have had a romantic relationship with her in this timeline.

  Exploring the upstairs rooms, the party discovers bedrooms that seem to belong to alternate versions of themselves:
  Helmut's room is neat and well maintained.
  Kallista's room is a complete mess, hiding several love poems dedicated to Mira.
  Phirina's room contains a pouch marked with the Snake symbol.

  Combined with the previous discoveries, the party now possesses all four symbols:
  Tree
  Owl
  Snake
  Key

  The possibility emerges that they are not forgetting memories, but stepping into realities where different versions of themselves already existed.

  The Constellation Puzzle
  While the others sleep, Phirina spends the night experimenting with Elira's constellation map and the four symbolic objects.

  Correctly placing them reveals hidden magical energy and causes a Mage Tower to appear simultaneously on both the map and within the city.

  The Mage Tower
  From outside it appears to be nothing more than a tiny abandoned shack.

  Inside, however, it unfolds into an immense magical tower preserved outside normal space.

  The party accidentally awakens an ancient golem, which ignores combat entirely and instead asks each character a philosophical question reflecting their deepest values.

  Before judging their answers, time freezes.

  The Temporal Agent
  A chained temporal figure appears.

  The Agent claims:
  They have been imprisoned inside the tower.
  Reality is collapsing.
  The party seems naturally resistant to temporal changes because of their unusual stubbornness and strength of will.
  The source of the anomaly lies within the forest ruins, where the original breach first opened.

  The Agent asks the party to investigate the ruins.

  Although sympathetic, the Agent deliberately hides their own responsibility for the catastrophe.

  The party agrees to investigate the ruins, but refuses to help free the prisoner.

  The Last Oath

  Bag of Holding

  Supplemental current-state instruction
  Ignore one-use items because they were spent in later play.
  Preserve that Soren has the Lantern of Lost Souls.
  Preserve the persistent weapon effects currently associated with Kallista and Helmut.

  Ending
  As the party leaves the tower, Perry suddenly cries for help.
