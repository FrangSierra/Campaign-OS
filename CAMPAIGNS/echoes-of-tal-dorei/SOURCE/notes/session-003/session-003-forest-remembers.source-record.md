  Session 3 – The Forest Remembers (Summary)

  Purpose
  The party begins investigating the source of the temporal anomalies inside ancient forest ruins. They discover that the mysterious prisoner cannot be fully trusted and prove themselves through four trials designed by the ancient Keepers.

  Sadek's Fate
  Following Perry into the forest, the party finds Sadek Bronzebeard aging at an impossible speed.
  He explains:
  - Time itself is broken inside the forest.
  - "The forest remembers things reality forgot."
  Moments later, reality tears open into a temporal portal.
  The party manages to pull Sadek free before he disappears forever, but the damage has already been done. Sadek survives as an impossibly old dwarf, no longer capable of fighting.
  Unable to continue, he entrusts Perry to the party. Rather than leaving him behind, the party decides to bring Sadek with them into the ruins, with Kallista carrying him throughout the expedition.
  Perry cryptically remarks:
  "He tried three times."
  The meaning remains unknown.

  The Forest Ruins
  The party reaches enormous underground ruins protected by an ancient stone gate.
  An inscription reads:
  Four may enter.
  One alone shall fail.
  Memory opens.
  Conviction guides.
  Strength endures.
  Knowledge humbles.
  Inside they discover a circular chamber dominated by a massive six-armed stone guardian surrounding an ancient fountain.
  Each completed trial causes one arm of the statue to break away.
  Every failed trial leaves an arm intact, making the guardian stronger if it awakens later.

  The Four Trials
  Kallista — Trial of Strength
  Kallista must push an enormous stone sphere through an ancient corridor.
  The challenge is not raw strength but perseverance.
  Even collapsing at the final push is considered success because she refused to give up.
  Her reward is only a single gold coin beneath the stone, accompanied by the inscription:
  "We knew you would move a mountain for a single coin."

  Soren — Trial of Compassion
  Soren is presented with two dying people and only enough healing to save one.
  Rather than choosing, he becomes frustrated with constantly being forced to decide the fate of others and throws the healing vial away.
  At that moment, Shar, the Goddess of Darkness, appears before him.
  She offers him:
  - Greater power.
  - Freedom from his suffering.
  - Fewer moral chains.
  In exchange, she asks for memories, not devotion.
  Soren accepts.
  Shar removes the emotions tied to one of his most painful memories: being beaten by the people of his village until Sister Dorota rescued him. The memory itself remains, but all the feelings attached to it vanish.
  As the pact is sealed:
  - His white clerical garments become black.
  - His holy symbol transforms into Shar's symbol.
  This change is permanent, and other priests and religious figures will recognize his new faith in future sessions.

  Helmut — Trial of Courage
  Helmut faces a burning village while his helmet rests safely out of reach.
  He must choose between retrieving the helmet or rescuing trapped civilians.
  The trial teaches that courage comes from the person, not the helmet.
  Saving the civilians completes the trial.

  Phirina — Trial of Knowledge
  Inside an infinite library, Phirina is asked why reality is changing.
  Every intelligent answer proves incomplete.
  The trial only ends once she admits:
  "I don't know."
  The lesson:
  "Knowledge begins where certainty ends."

  Hidden Discoveries
  Temporal Hideout
  Evidence reveals that someone secretly studied the Rune long before reality began collapsing.
  Notes suggest:
  - The Rune can rewrite reality.
  - The author wanted to improve the world by creating a better timeline.
  - They believed only one timeline deserved to exist.
  This strongly implies the chained Temporal Agent is hiding the truth and was never simply a victim.

  Keeper Cache
  The party finds supplies left behind by the ancient Keepers, including four magical crystal coins capable of rerolling one failed skill check each.
  A journal warns:
  "The Rune rewards power.
  The world rewards wisdom.
  Learn the difference."

  Ending
  After all four trials are completed, the six-armed stone guardian awakens, with its remaining strength determined by how many trials the party succeeded in.
  The session ends just as combat begins, leaving the battle for the next session.
  The party reaches Level 3.

  Current Party
  The group now travels with:
  - Helmut
  - Soren (now a cleric of Shar)
  - Kallista
  - Phirina
  - Sadek Bronzebeard (extremely aged and unable to fight)
  - Perry (comic relief and Sadek's loyal parrot)

  Major Revelations
  - Sadek survives but is permanently aged and now accompanies the party.
  - Perry officially joins the group.
  - Soren abandons Sarenrae and forms a pact with Shar, sacrificing the emotions tied to one of his defining childhood memories.
  - The chained Temporal Agent is almost certainly hiding their true role in the catastrophe.
  - The Rune is capable of rewriting reality itself.
  - The ancient Keepers built the trials to judge character rather than strength.
  - The Stone Guardian has awakened, and the next session begins with the party's first major boss encounter.
