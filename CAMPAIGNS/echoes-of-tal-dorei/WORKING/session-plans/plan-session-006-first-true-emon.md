# Session Plan - Session 6 - First True Emon

## Human Brief

Use Emon as the party's permanent home city after the prologue. Shift hard away from time-travel-centered play and into grounded investigation, political intrigue, disappearances, hidden organizations, ruins, dwarven history, moral decisions, character interactions, recurring NPCs, and regular returns to Emon. The first post-prologue session should be relatively relaxed: describe the true city, let the party split naturally, seed individualized hooks, introduce Sadek's retired-investigator cousin Brann Copperthumb, and end by handing the party a scholar mystery whose real question is not "Who killed him?" but "What actually happened?"

## Planning Constraints

- Do not make time travel, alternate timelines, or reality manipulation the main subject of the new campaign.
- Sessions 1 through 5 remain shared history for the party, Sadek, and Perry, but public Emon should feel stable and normal.
- Emon must feel inhabited, layered, and worth caring about.
- Combat should support the investigation rather than replace it.
- Not every mystery should point to one master conspiracy.
- The first true Emon session should establish recurring NPC anchors and a repeatable campaign rhythm.
- The current approved party level is `3`, so threat and reward pacing should stay Tier 1.

## Current Context

- The party is back at Emerald Outpost on the true road to Emon after the prologue reset.
- Sadek and Perry continue with the party into the restored timeline.
- The party remembers the collapsed branch, but public world history does not.
- The largest current canon mysteries concern Arven, the Time Agency, and possible aftereffects of the collapsed branch.
- For this draft, use the `Campaign-specific Emon` dial:
  - keep Tal'Dorei and Emon structure
  - keep Lyceum, Council, districts, and city identity
  - keep deeper Vox Machina-era canon as background texture unless it directly improves play
- This plan intentionally does not foreground the Time Agency.

## Core Session Idea

The first true Emon session is about emotional and civic re-entry. The party finally reaches the real city they were denied, sees why it matters, and immediately begins forming attachments through different corners of urban life: academia, temples, markets, labor, and local investigation. Their first case begins not because destiny chooses them, but because they are curious, available, and just strange enough to notice when a "locked-room murder" is actually a dead scholar's final attempt to recruit the right kind of problem-solvers.

## Why This Session Matters Now

- It redefines the campaign tone after the prologue.
- It converts Emon from abstract destination into lived space.
- It gives each player character an immediate city-facing angle.
- It introduces at least two recurring NPC anchors.
- It launches a mystery structure based on interpretation, motive, and hidden context rather than simple culprit-hunting.

## Candidate Hooks

- `Primary hook`: a respected Lyceum-connected scholar dies under impossible circumstances, and the scene appears staged.
- `Phirina hook`: casual Lyceum gossip about the scholar's disappearance or suspicious death.
- `Helmut hook`: an "Investigators Wanted" notice disappears from the mercenary board just as he notices it.
- `Kallista hook`: a folded note appears in her pocket from a child information broker named Ivy.
- `Soren hook`: a temple priest quietly warns him to cover his Shar symbol, establishing that some doors in Emon open only with discretion.
- `Sadek/Brann hook`: Sadek introduces the party to Brann Copperthumb before they disperse too far into the city.

## Recommended Session Structure

### Scene 1 - Arrival At The Real Emon

Goal:

- make the city itself feel like a payoff

Recommended texture:

- huge walls
- layered districts
- crowded gates
- guards and tariffs
- visible magic
- skyship movement overhead
- canals and bridges
- market noise and temple bells
- scholars, laborers, nobles, dockhands, and travelers all in the same civic organism

No roll is necessary here. This should be pure framing and invitation.

End beat:

- Ask each character what they do first.

### Scene 2 - Split Character Introductions To The City

#### Phirina - The Lyceum Rumor

Phirina heads to the Alabaster Lyceum or its public-facing grounds in the Erudite Quarter.

What she gets:

- a sense of status, wealth, and guarded knowledge
- gossip that Master Archivist `Oren Vestrel` was found dead or has vanished after obsessive research into old civic records, foundations, or pre-republic survey notes
- nobody formally hires her
- curiosity is the bait

Suggested mechanics:

- no roll to hear the basic gossip
- `DC 10 Charisma (Persuasion)` or `DC 10 Intelligence (Investigation)` to learn the scholar had recently become erratic
- `DC 15 Intelligence (History)` or `DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana)` to learn his work touched old transit routes, warded archives, or dwarven-era substructures beneath Emon

#### Helmut - The Vanishing Notice

Helmut checks the mercenary boards in or near the Military District.

What he gets:

- the ordinary city is full of mundane work
- escort jobs, caravan posts, rat problems, neighborhood patrols
- one posting stands out: `Investigators Wanted`
- before he can take it, a Watch functionary or messenger removes it

Suggested mechanics:

- no roll to see the notice
- `DC 10 Wisdom (Perception)` to catch the badge, insignia, or route of the person removing it
- `DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion)` or `DC 15 Wisdom (Insight)` to learn the Watch is looking into a scholar death it does not fully understand

#### Kallista - Ivy's Invitation

Kallista moves through Abdar's Promenade looking for profit, work, or exploitable opportunity.

What she gets:

- no theft
- a folded note slipped into a pocket
- message: `You walk like someone looking for work.`
- later, she spots Ivy on a rooftop, a stall awning, or a canal wall eating something sweet and watching her reaction

Ivy's role in this session:

- not quest giver
- not all-knowing
- fast, observant, amused by adults
- knows which district is whispering about the dead scholar
- can point toward Brann, the Watch, or the scholar's rented rooms depending on how the party proceeds

Suggested mechanics:

- no roll to receive the note
- `DC 15 Wisdom (Perception)` to spot Ivy before she vanishes
- rooftop pursuit should not gate the introduction; failure means Ivy chooses the meeting place instead
- `Influence` with Ivy should respond strongly to food, curiosity, and honesty instead of coin alone

#### Soren - The Temple Warning

Soren visits a temple in the Temple District.

What he gets:

- a subtle but immediate social boundary
- a priest or temple attendant notices the Shar symbol and quietly tells him to cover it
- no accusation, no public scene, no immediate enemy
- Emon is not safe for careless religious disclosure

Suggested mechanics:

- no roll for the warning
- `DC 15 Wisdom (Insight)` to realize the priest is more afraid than angry
- if Soren pushes conversation, use `Influence` rather than a flat reaction roll

### Scene 3 - Brann Copperthumb

Before the party fully disperses or once they reconvene, Sadek asks them to meet someone important.

Brann's draft role:

- Sadek's older cousin
- retired private investigator
- well known across Emon
- locally reputed to have never failed to resolve a crime
- physically limited, mentally sharp
- calm, dry, organized, never theatrical
- loves birdwatching
- perfect for recurring debrief scenes and case reframing

First meeting beats:

- orderly office or home archive
- labeled case files, clean shelving, no conspiracy wall
- bird field guides, feeder notes, or sketches are tucked among the case files, revealing a gentle personal obsession rather than eccentric chaos
- Brann sees the party, looks at Sadek, and says: `...They're alive?`
- Sadek: `Somehow.`
- Brann files their names because he assumes they will return

Function in campaign rhythm:

- recurring mentor
- trusted local lens on Emon
- does not travel with the party
- can recommend lines of inquiry, old contacts, private-sector gossip, and procedural caution
- should feel impressive because of his reputation, but his physical limitations and deliberate noninterference keep the case in the party's hands

### Scene 4 - The Watch Arrives

While the party is with Brann, a City Watch representative arrives looking for him about the scholar case.

Recommended NPC:

- `Inspector Mara Thessel`
  - competent, tired, not corrupt
  - respects Brann's reputation
  - practical enough to use unofficial help if the formal process is stuck

Beat:

- Brann declines because of his knees
- Brann points the case at the party with a dry line instead of a heroic speech
- this formally converges all four split hooks

### Scene 5 - The Scholar Scene

Recommended location:

- a rented study townhouse in the Central District near the Erudite Quarter

Why this works:

- it avoids making the Lyceum itself instantly incompetent
- it lets the scene feel private, curated, and stage-managed

Surface read:

- locked-room murder
- broken window
- burned papers
- blood
- two teacups
- strange symbols
- missing notebook

Actual truth:

- the scholar died from magical exhaustion, stress, or backlash tied to a dangerous solo experiment or ward
- much of the scene was arranged after he knew he was dying or expected he might die
- the false murder tableau was designed to steer curious investigators toward the right questions and away from the wrong authorities

Essential clue logic:

- broken window suggests escape, but was broken from the inside
- two teacups suggest a guest, but one is old, dusty, or performative
- burned papers look like destroyed evidence, but are failed theories the scholar wanted discarded
- blood suggests violence, but its pattern implies collapse during magical exertion
- missing notebook matters more than any apparent attacker

### Scene 6 - What The Scene Was Really For

The turning point should happen when the party stops asking `who killed him` and starts asking `why construct this story`.

Probable answer:

- the scholar believed something under or within Emon's civic history was being quietly buried
- he did not trust either normal academic channels or parts of the Watch to follow it safely
- he wanted investigators who were not yet captured by local politics

Recommended future-facing reveal for the notebook trail:

- not "reality is breaking again"
- instead, one of these grounded directions:
  - hidden municipal records tied to vanished underworks
  - a smuggling route using old maintenance tunnels
  - a noble-backed land grab over buried ruins
  - a reconstruction-era contract cover-up connected to restricted archives
  - a dwarven survey map that should not exist in the hands of its current owner

Recommended default for this draft:

- `old maintenance tunnels and erased survey records beneath Emon`
  - supports investigation
  - supports dwarven history
  - supports future city adventures
  - can later intersect with nobles, smugglers, builders, or secret societies
  - approved direction for the next revision unless later discussion changes it

### Scene 7 - Optional Light Combat

Combat is optional and should happen only if pacing needs a spike.

Best use:

- rival scavengers, hired toughs, or a panicked arcane safeguard arrive while the party is processing the truth of the scene

Level 3 recommendation:

- one short fight, not a set piece
- examples:
  - `1 bandit captain` plus `2 bandits`
  - `3 thugs`
  - `2 guards` plus `1 cultist fanatic` only if there is a specific story reason
  - one weak magical construct or animated object if the scholar scene needs a warding complication

Combat objective should be narrative:

- protect the notebook
- stop evidence theft
- prevent a witness from dying
- hold the scene until Brann or the Watch arrives

### Scene 8 - Session End State

Ideal closing note:

- the party understands what happened to the scholar, but not yet who else cared about his work
- Brann recognizes they can actually do the job
- Ivy now knows they are interesting
- the Watch now tolerates or relies on them in a limited way
- Emon feels like a place with multiple doors now open

## NPC Candidates

### Brann Copperthumb

- role: retired investigator and recurring mentor
- tie: Sadek's cousin and the party's first trustworthy city anchor
- pressure: asks sharp questions and notices omissions without dominating the room
- signature texture: birdwatcher, precise observer, and famous private detective whose record makes other people lower their voice when he is mentioned

### Ivy

- role: street-level information broker
- tie: Abdar's Promenade, rooftops, couriers, market rumor
- pressure: useful but unowned, sometimes exaggerates, always prices things in memorable ways

### Inspector Mara Thessel

- role: City Watch liaison
- tie: procedural access to the scholar case
- pressure: wants answers fast and dislikes amateur chaos, but she is not stupid

### Oren Vestrel

- role: dead scholar whose scene launches the mystery
- tie: Lyceum-adjacent research into old Emon records and buried infrastructure
- pressure: even dead, he controls the party's first reading of the case through how he staged it

### Assistant Archivist Lenora Vale

- role: living academic contact
- tie: Lyceum gossip, access limits, and bureaucratic friction
- pressure: wants the truth, but fears the institutional cost of being seen helping

### Brother Caradoc Mere

- role: discreet temple contact for Soren
- tie: Temple District and the social danger around Shar
- pressure: can warn Soren about how Emon reads faith, but will recoil if pushed too publicly

## Suggested Mechanics And DCs

### Arrival And Split Scenes

- basic rumor exposure: no roll
- useful district rumor: `DC 10`
- hidden but fair social or observational detail: `DC 15`
- elite or buried institutional detail: `DC 20`

### Crime Scene Clues

Do not gate the central turn of the mystery behind one failed roll.

Auto or `DC 10` clues:

- the room was arranged to be read
- the window broke from the inside
- some burned papers were discarded deliberately rather than snatched in panic

`DC 15` clarifying clues:

- blood pattern suggests collapse during magical exertion rather than a weapon strike
- one teacup is part of a performed scene rather than evidence of a recent guest
- symbols in the study are note-markers, ciphers, or memory anchors rather than cult graffiti

`DC 20` deeper shortcut clues:

- the scholar expected institutional interference
- the hidden notebook compartment or secondary routing clue
- the precise connection between his research and erased civic survey records

### Social Defaults

- Ivy should usually begin `Indifferent` but be easily shifted with the right offer
- Brann begins effectively `Friendly` but not impressed
- Mara begins `Indifferent` and becomes `Hostile` only if the party contaminates the scene or lies badly
- temple contact begins `Hesitant`

### Reward Guidance

Tier 1 reward style should stay modest.

Better rewards than a raw magic weapon here:

- a City Watch consultation token
- a Lyceum visitor pass with limits
- access to a restricted file room
- Brann's private case index for one district
- a map fragment of old Emon tunnels
- one or two useful consumables

If a magic item is used, prefer:

- `Common` or `Uncommon`
- investigative utility over damage increase

Examples:

- goggles or lens for detail work
- a single-use ward token
- a charm against hostile entry
- a discreet sending or signal tool

## Continuity Risks

- Do not let the scholar mystery immediately become another reality-collapse plot.
- Do not let Brann overshadow the party by solving the case from his chair.
- Do not make Ivy too omniscient on first appearance.
- Be careful with Shar reactions in Emon: social pressure is stronger than instant open persecution.
- If the city intro runs long, compress split scenes before the Watch arrival rather than cutting Brann entirely.

## Recommended Default Version

If we want the cleanest first pass, use this:

1. vivid Emon arrival
2. one short spotlight beat for each PC
3. Brann introduction over tea
4. Watch arrival and handoff
5. locked-room scene
6. realization that the scholar staged the mystery
7. optional short combat or evidence-race complication
8. close on the missing notebook trail into old Emon civic secrets
