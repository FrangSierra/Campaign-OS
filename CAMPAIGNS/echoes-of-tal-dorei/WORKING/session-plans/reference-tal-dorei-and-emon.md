# Tal'Dorei and Emon Planning Reference

## Purpose

Operational setting primer for planning future sessions in the true Tal'Dorei timeline.

This document is not canon for our campaign by itself. It is a planning aid built from Critical Role setting material so session drafts can feel grounded in Tal'Dorei and especially Emon.

## Scope Note

- Use this as inspiration and constraint, not as a prison.
- If our campaign deliberately diverges, the session plan should say so explicitly.
- When this document conflicts with established campaign canon, our campaign canon wins.

## Source Baseline

- Critical Role Wiki summaries for Tal'Dorei, Emon, the Alabaster Lyceum, the Republic of Tal'Dorei, the Tal'Dorei Council, and the League of Miracles
- D&D Beyond overview of `Tal'Dorei Campaign Setting Reborn`

## Tal'Dorei Anchors

- Tal'Dorei is the human name for the continent; its Elvish name is `Gwessar`, "Fields of Joy".
- The continent mixes mountains, plains, forests, jungles, and major political regions rather than functioning as one monoculture.
- The Republic of Tal'Dorei sits on the continent and uses Emon as its capital.
- The Bladeshimmer Shoreline is the key coastal region for Emon-facing stories.
- Emerald Outpost is a trade and military waystation between Syngorn and Emon, which makes it a natural gateway into the city.

## Emon At A Glance

- Emon is the capital of the Republic of Tal'Dorei and the largest city on the continent.
- It is known as the `City of Fellowship` and the `Jewel of Tal'Dorei`.
- Emon is a political, economic, educational, and religious hub, not just an adventuring launch point.
- The city is heavily fortified, with a thick outer wall around the city and a secondary defensive wall around the Cloudtop District.
- Canals called the `Ozmit Waterways` cut through the city and help move people and goods.
- Emon also supports skyship access, which helps sell the sense that this is a major magical metropolis.

## District Cheat Sheet

### Abdar's Promenade

- The great open-air market district on the east side of the city.
- Best for merchants, rumors, hired work, fences, smugglers, street culture, and chance meetings.
- Canon touchstones include `Gilmore's Glorious Goods` and the `Laughing Lamia Inn`.
- Good session uses:
  - black-market trade
  - artifact theft
  - merchant fraud
  - information brokers
  - disguised surveillance

### Central District

- Main residential and merchant-class zone.
- Dense neighborhoods, taverns, inns, guildhalls, parks, and everyday city life.
- Good session uses:
  - witnesses
  - apartment searches
  - neighborhood tensions
  - recurring civilians
  - mundane mysteries that become personal

### Cloudtop District

- Government and noble quarter at the city's center.
- Home to the Council's palace, nobles, military planning, major arcane figures, and the skyship port.
- Best for political intrigue, formal audiences, elite crimes, and hidden state pressure.
- If using the post-Conclave rebuilt era, this district still carries the memory of devastation and reconstruction.

### Temple District

- Religious center of Emon.
- Major temples center on Prime Deity worship, especially Erathis and Bahamut, with other Prime faiths also present.
- Good session uses:
  - moral pressure
  - missing clergy
  - relic custody disputes
  - hidden heresies
  - social danger for Shar-linked material

### Erudite Quarter

- Higher education district and one of the strongest anchors for Phirina.
- Home to the `Alabaster Lyceum`, the most prestigious place of learning in Tal'Dorei.
- The Lyceum is expensive, elite, and tied to scholarship, politics, magical study, and artifact research.
- The `Traverse Junction` provides teleportation-circle infrastructure and makes the district feel globally connected.
- Good session uses:
  - scholar disappearances
  - academic rivalries
  - censored research
  - sealed archives
  - magical fraud

### Military District

- Barracks, training grounds, and guard infrastructure.
- The `Arms of Emon` anchor local order here.
- Good session uses:
  - mercenary boards
  - watch politics
  - prisoner transfers
  - confiscated evidence
  - corruption inside security structures

## Political and Faction Anchors

### Republic of Tal'Dorei

- Tal'Dorei is a republic with Emon as capital.
- The sovereign line gave way to council rule, so the state can support both civic pride and bureaucratic tension.
- This makes it easy to run investigations that touch public duty, regional interests, and competing city voices.

### Tal'Dorei Council

- The Council oversees the Republic's major functions.
- Its domains include Development, Arcana, Law, Commerce, Information, Defense, and War when needed.
- This is extremely useful for campaign planning because it gives us ready-made pressure centers for investigations:
  - Development for rebuilding, contracts, and land deals
  - Arcana for magical law, sanctioned research, and dangerous relics
  - Law for courts, warrants, and jurisdiction fights
  - Commerce for trade fraud, shortages, and guild pressure
  - Information for diplomats, spies, and quiet manipulation
  - Defense for the guard, military readiness, and urban security

### League of Miracles

- Strong optional long-burn faction if we want post-Chroma political intrigue.
- Publicly, they are miracle workers and reconstruction specialists with a glowing image.
- Privately, they are described as a mage consortium seeking power over Tal'Dorei.
- They have tension with the Alabaster Lyceum and Arcana-aligned institutions.
- They are ideal for:
  - reconstruction corruption
  - magical monopolies
  - contract fraud
  - secret experiments
  - respectable public faces hiding coercive power

## Alabaster Lyceum Planning Notes

- The Lyceum is the top place of learning in Tal'Dorei.
- It serves arcane study, historical research, divine-artifact scholarship, politics, and elite education.
- It is expensive enough that status and money matter there.
- Its architecture and magical lighting support scenes that feel formal, ancient, and prestigious rather than cozy.
- For Phirina scenes, the Lyceum works best when it offers:
  - rumor before mandate
  - access with strings attached
  - scholars with agendas
  - restricted knowledge that somebody already died, vanished, or lied for

## Best-Fit Uses For Our Campaign Direction

- Grounded investigations in a living city
- Political intrigue without requiring world-ending stakes
- Strange disappearances and secret societies
- Fraud, smuggling, theft, academic rivalry, and noble pressure
- Ruins and old Tal'Dorei history accessed through city institutions rather than random wilderness hooks
- Recurring NPC networks tied to place:
  - Brann through old investigations and retired contacts
  - Ivy through rooftops, markets, couriers, and street rumor
  - Lyceum scholars through the Erudite Quarter
  - Watch, merchants, temples, and taverns as repeat anchors

## Timeline Dials

Use these intentionally when planning:

- `Light-touch Emon`
  - Use the city layout, districts, institutions, and atmosphere.
  - Minimize deep Vox Machina aftermath.

- `Post-Conclave Emon`
  - Keep visible scars, rebuild politics, League of Miracles influence, and civic trauma.
  - Strongest choice for intrigue, construction scams, and power shifts.

- `Campaign-specific Emon`
  - Keep Tal'Dorei structure and city identity, but replace any unwanted canon pressure with our own mysteries.
  - Best default if we want the feel of Critical Role without inheriting every major canon thread.

## Planning Guidance For Session Drafts

- Make Emon feel crowded, layered, and inhabited by people with routines.
- Not every clue should point to a world-level plot.
- Repeat places on purpose so the city becomes emotionally legible.
- Use districts to differentiate mystery texture:
  - Promenade for noise and commerce
  - Central for domestic witness work
  - Cloudtop for power and secrecy
  - Temple District for morality and public faith
  - Erudite Quarter for scholarship and magical research
  - Military District for procedure, force, and containment
- If a draft leans too hard into apocalypse, reset toward civic stakes, personal costs, and institutional friction.
