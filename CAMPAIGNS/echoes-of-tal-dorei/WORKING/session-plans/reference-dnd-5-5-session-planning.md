# D&D 5.5 Session Planning Reference

## Purpose

Operational rules primer for planning sessions, clues, DCs, NPC interactions, hazards, combat scenes, and item rewards in a `Dungeons & Dragons 5.5` campaign.

This document uses the 2024 D&D rules baseline on D&D Beyond. It is for planning support, not for replacing the table's judgment in play.

## Source Baseline

- `D&D Beyond Basic Rules` (2024)
- `Rules Glossary`
- `SRD v5.2.1`

## Core Planning Assumptions

- The game is organized around three main pillars: social interaction, exploration, and combat.
- Use a D20 Test when the outcome has both a meaningful chance of failure and a narratively interesting result.
- The DM sets DCs unless a rule already provides one.
- Exception-based design matters: specific features, spells, items, and monster abilities override general rules.

## Default DC Ladder

Use the official DC ladder as the starting point:

- `5` very easy
- `10` easy
- `15` medium
- `20` hard
- `25` very hard
- `30` nearly impossible

## Practical Planning Heuristics

These are repo recommendations built on top of the official ladder:

- `DC 10`
  - ordinary rumor gathering
  - spotting the obvious inconsistency
  - noticing a tail in a quiet room

- `DC 15`
  - core investigative work
  - reading a misleading scene correctly
  - identifying a concealed but fair clue
  - following city tracks before they go cold

- `DC 20`
  - guarded records
  - well-executed lies
  - hidden compartments
  - specialized scholarship under pressure

- `DC 25+`
  - elite cover-ups
  - deeply buried secrets
  - extreme stunts
  - information that should usually require setup, leverage, or multiple clues

## Investigation-Facing Actions

### Search

- `Search` is the right action when a character tries to notice something not obvious.
- The default pairings are:
  - `Insight` for a creature's state of mind
  - `Medicine` for ailment or cause of death
  - `Perception` for concealed creatures or objects
  - `Survival` for tracks or food

### Study

- `Study` is the right action when a character is reasoning about a clue, text, memory, trap, cipher, or body of knowledge.
- Strong pairings for investigation planning:
  - `Arcana` for spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, planes, and magical traditions
  - `History` for historical people, events, wars, and ancient civilizations
  - `Investigation` for traps, ciphers, riddles, and gadgetry
  - `Nature` for terrain, weather, flora, and some creature knowledge

### Passive Perception

- Passive Perception is `10 + Perception bonus`.
- Increase it by `5` if the creature would have Advantage on Perception checks.
- Decrease it by `5` if the creature would have Disadvantage.
- Use it for ambient noticing, tailing, overheard detail, and whether a hidden element is detected without declared searching.

## Social Interaction Defaults

### Influence

- `Influence` is the main action for persuading, deceiving, intimidating, amusing, or coaxing a creature.
- No check is needed if the creature is already willing.
- No check is needed if the request is flatly unacceptable.
- If the creature is hesitant, use an ability check.

### Attitude Framework

- `Friendly`: the character has Advantage on checks to influence the creature.
- `Indifferent`: default neutral stance.
- `Hostile`: the character has Disadvantage on checks to influence the creature.

### Influence DC

- The default DC for a hesitant target is `15` or the target's Intelligence score, whichever is higher.

### Default Influence Skill Pairings

- `Deception` for lies
- `Intimidation` for pressure
- `Performance` for amusement or dramatic sway
- `Persuasion` for sincere convincing
- `Animal Handling` for gently coaxing a Beast or Monstrosity

## Combat Planning Defaults

### Encounter Flow

- Combat begins with positions, then initiative, then turns in rounds.
- A round is about `6 seconds`.
- If a combatant is surprised when combat starts, that combatant has Disadvantage on its Initiative roll.

### What A Turn Usually Contains

- move up to Speed
- one action
- maybe one Bonus Action if a feature, spell, or other rule grants it
- maybe one Reaction between turns, refreshed at the start of the creature's next turn

### Useful Practical Rules

- Characters can break up movement before and after actions.
- One object interaction is usually free on a turn.
- A second object interaction usually needs the `Utilize` action.
- Opportunity Attacks trigger when a creature leaves reach using movement, an action, a Bonus Action, a Reaction, or one of its speeds.
- `Disengage`, teleportation, or forced movement prevent most opportunity attacks.

### Cover

- `Half Cover`: `+2` AC and Dexterity saves
- `Three-Quarters Cover`: `+5` AC and Dexterity saves
- `Total Cover`: cannot be targeted directly

### Hiding

- `Hide` usually requires a successful `DC 15 Dexterity (Stealth)` check.
- The creature must be heavily obscured or behind three-quarters or total cover and out of enemy line of sight.

### Concentration

- If a concentrating creature takes damage, the save DC is `10` or half the damage taken, whichever is higher, up to `30`.
- Concentration also ends if the creature starts another concentration effect, becomes incapacitated, or dies.

## Rest and Pacing Defaults

- `Short Rest`: `1 hour`
- `Long Rest`: at least `8 hours`, including `6` hours of sleep and no more than `2` hours of light activity
- A Long Rest cannot be started again until `16` hours after the previous one finished

Planning implication:

- Investigation-heavy city sessions can credibly hold one main action sequence and one combat without forcing multiple rests.
- If you want resource pressure, limit safe rest windows instead of inflating encounter count.

## Travel and Urban Search Defaults

For urban overland movement in the DM's Toolbox:

- maximum pace: `Normal`
- encounter distance: `2d6 x 10 feet`
- foraging DC: `20`
- navigation DC: `15`
- search DC: `15`

Use these sparingly in city play. They are best for:

- chases
- rooftop patrol patterns
- fog, smoke, night watch, and alley ambush distance
- sustained searches across districts

## Traps and Hazard Defaults

- Use traps sparingly.
- Favor traps that create quick problem-solving, teamwork, or scene escalation instead of pure punishment.
- Example tier scaling from the Basic Rules shows low-level deadly traps around save DC `13`, then scaling to `15`, `17`, and `19` at higher level bands.

Planning implication:

- In an investigative campaign, traps should usually guard secrets, evidence, vaults, labs, and panic routes.
- Avoid stuffing ordinary urban mysteries with dungeon-density trap logic.

## Fear and Mental Stress

Optional rules support fear and mental stress with sample Wisdom save DCs:

- `10` for a mild but frightening supernatural shock
- `15` for intense personalized terror
- `20` for overwhelming cosmic or abyssal horror

Planning implication:

- Use these for cults, cursed archives, forbidden experiments, and Shar-adjacent scenes only when tone supports it.
- Do not overuse them in grounded civic mysteries.

## Magic Item Defaults

### Design Philosophy

- Magic items are prizes, not required math patches.
- Characters and monsters are expected to function without them.

### Rarity Value Anchors

- `Common`: `100 GP`
- `Uncommon`: `400 GP`
- `Rare`: `4,000 GP`
- `Very Rare`: `40,000 GP`
- `Legendary`: `200,000 GP`
- `Artifact`: priceless

### Award Pace By Tier

Typical total party awards by the Basic Rules:

- levels `1-4`: `11` items total
- levels `5-10`: `34`
- levels `11-16`: `30`
- levels `17-20`: `25`

### Practical Campaign Recommendation

For our Emon investigation campaign:

- Tier 1 should lean mostly `Common` and `Uncommon`
- a `Rare` item should feel like a real event
- use consumables, documents, favors, and situational tools more often than constant-stat boosters
- prefer items that open choices, access, leverage, or identity over raw damage inflation

## Repo Planning Recommendations

These are not official rules; they are our default prep style:

- Do not gate core clues behind a single failed roll.
- Essential clue: auto-reveal or `DC 10`
- Clarifying clue: `DC 10-15`
- Deep or dangerous shortcut clue: `DC 15-20`
- Secret backdoor or elite conspiracy clue: `DC 20+`
- Social scenes should use attitude plus leverage, not just one flat Persuasion roll.
- Combat should reinforce the mystery:
  - protect a witness
  - stop a courier
  - survive an ambush
  - contain a summoned thing
  - chase an escaping culprit

## Default Rule Baseline For This Repo

Until we decide otherwise, session planning should assume:

- 2024 D&D Beyond Basic Rules structure
- Rules Glossary terminology
- SRD v5.2.1 compatibility
- Tal'Dorei third-party content only when we explicitly choose to use it
